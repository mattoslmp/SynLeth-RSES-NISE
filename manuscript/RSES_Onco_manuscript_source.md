# RSES-Onco: a coverage-aware framework for prioritizing cancer vulnerabilities from hidden functional enzyme backups

**Author:** Leandro de Mattos Pereira  
**Affiliation:** Databiomics, Laboratory of Bioinformatics and Data Science, WBPereira, Itaperuna, Rio de Janeiro, Brazil

## Framework scope

RSES-Onco integrates eligible and observed evidence across tumor events, conditional dependency, selectivity, expression compensation, functional microniches, validation/tractability and supporting biochemical, structural, regulatory and pharmacological context. The implemented workflow distinguishes candidate-universe membership, computational prioritization, nominal statistical support, FDR-supported results, external validation and clinical evidence.

## Coverage-aware scoring

For eligible component $i$, weight $w_i$, observed indicator $m_i$ and normalized component value $x_i$:

$S_{observed} = \frac{\sum_i w_i m_i x_i}{\sum_i w_i m_i}$

$Coverage = \frac{\sum_i w_i m_i}{\sum_{i \in E} w_i}$

$S_{adjusted} = S_{observed} \times Coverage = \frac{\sum_i w_i m_i x_i}{\sum_{i \in E} w_i}$

Missing evidence is not converted to zero. Non-eligible domains do not enter the eligible denominator. Technical failure and insufficient sample size remain explicit missingness states rather than negative biological evidence.

## Evidence interpretation

- Correlation does not by itself establish transcriptional compensation.
- Conditional dependency is distinguished from general essentiality.
- STRING combined evidence is not treated as direct experimental interaction.
- JASPAR/FIMO motif occurrence is a promoter-sequence prediction, not direct TF binding.
- Structural-model availability is not equivalent to biochemical validation.
- Computational tractability and pharmacological association are not clinical efficacy.
- A high RSES-Onco score is a prioritization result and is not automatically a discovery, synthetic-lethal interaction or therapeutic target.
